ROOTS OF QUADRATIC EQUATIONS USING GRAPH

Another way of solving quadratic equation is to solve it graphically. The roots of a quadratic equation are the x intercepts of the graph.
Example:

From the graph above, the x intercepts are −5 and −1
⇒the roots of the equation are −5 and −1

NB: There're three cases of finding roots of a quadratic equation graphically

  • A quadratic equation has one (1) roots if its graph has two x-intercepts
  • A quadratic equation has two (2) roots if its graph has two x-intercepts
  • A quadratic equation has no real solution if its graph has no x-intercepts
